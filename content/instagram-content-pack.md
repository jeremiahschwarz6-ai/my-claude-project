# Instagram Content Pack — Business & Money Niche

Starter content for a from-zero personal finance / money account. Everything here
is written to be posted as-is or filmed on a phone with no equipment.

**Strategy in one line:** post educational money content optimized for saves and
shares, 4–5x per week, in one consistent visual style, for 60 days before judging
results.

---

## Part 1: Ready-to-film Reel scripts

Film these as talking-head clips on your phone (front camera, good window light,
captions on). Each is 20–30 seconds. Read the hook looking at the lens, cut fast
between sentences, no intro, no outro fluff.

### Reel 1 — "The $0 start"
- **Hook (on-screen text + spoken):** "You don't need money to start making money. You need these 3 things."
- One: a skill someone will pay for — editing, writing, design, spreadsheets. Pick one, spend 2 weeks on YouTube learning it.
- Two: proof — do it free for 2 people, screenshot the results.
- Three: distribution — DM 10 small businesses a day showing that proof.
- **CTA:** "Comment 'START' and I'll send you the exact DM script I'd use."

### Reel 2 — "Broke habits"
- **Hook:** "5 habits keeping you broke that have nothing to do with how much you earn."
- 1. You budget after you spend, not before.
- 2. You save what's left over instead of saving first.
- 3. You upgrade your lifestyle every time your income goes up.
- 4. You avoid looking at your bank account when it's bad.
- 5. You treat 'investing' as something you'll do later.
- **CTA:** "Save this and check back in 30 days."

### Reel 3 — "The 50/30/20 remix"
- **Hook:** "Everyone teaches 50/30/20 wrong. Here's the version that actually works when you're broke."
- If rent alone eats 50%, the classic rule is useless. Do this instead: fix your four survival numbers first (rent, food, transport, phone).
- Whatever's left, split 80/20 — 80% to breathing room, 20% auto-transferred somewhere you can't see it.
- The goal at the start isn't wealth. It's ending the month above zero.
- **CTA:** "Follow — next video: where that 20% should actually go."

### Reel 4 — "One-minute money audit"
- **Hook:** "Open your banking app. This takes 60 seconds and will genuinely annoy you."
- Sort last month by merchant. Find the three you spent the most on that aren't rent or groceries.
- Multiply each by 12. Say the yearly number out loud.
- You're not cutting them all. You're cutting one. That's the whole exercise.
- **CTA:** "Comment the most embarrassing yearly number. I'll go first — mine was delivery apps: $2,100."

### Reel 5 — "Salary is a subscription"
- **Hook:** "Your salary is a subscription your employer can cancel anytime. Here's the unsubscribe insurance."
- One income = one point of failure. The fix isn't quitting — it's stacking.
- Layer 1: your job. Layer 2: one paid skill on the side (even $200/mo).
- Layer 3: that side money goes into index funds, not lifestyle.
- Three layers means no single cancellation ruins you.
- **CTA:** "Share this with someone who thinks their job is 'safe'."

## Part 2: Carousel — "7 Money Rules I Wish I Knew at 20"

Cover slide is being designed in Canva. Full slide text below — duplicate the
cover slide in Canva 8 times and swap in this text, keeping the same fonts/colors.

1. **Cover:** 7 Money Rules I Wish I Knew at 20 *(small subtext: "#4 changed everything")*
2. **Rule 1 — Pay yourself first.** Savings isn't what's left after spending. Spending is what's left after saving. Automate it on payday.
3. **Rule 2 — Your income is your #1 wealth tool.** Cutting lattes saves $5. Learning a skill that raises your income makes $500. Focus accordingly.
4. **Rule 3 — Lifestyle inflation is the silent killer.** Every raise you fully spend is a raise you never got. Bank at least half of every increase.
5. **Rule 4 — Time in the market beats timing the market.** $200/mo invested at 20 beats $600/mo started at 35. Boring index funds. Decades. That's the cheat code.
6. **Rule 5 — Debt has a hierarchy.** Credit card debt is an emergency. A reasonable mortgage isn't. Kill anything above ~8% interest before you invest a cent.
7. **Rule 6 — An emergency fund is what makes you brave.** 3 months of expenses in cash is what lets you quit bad jobs, take good risks, and sleep.
8. **Rule 7 — Nobody is coming to save you.** No employer, no government, no lottery. The day you fully accept that is the day your finances change.
9. **Final slide (CTA):** Save this for your future self. Follow @yourhandle — one practical money idea every day.

## Part 4: Caption formula

Every caption, same skeleton:

1. **First line = the hook restated** (it's what shows before "...more").
2. **2–4 short lines of added value** — one detail the video didn't cover.
3. **CTA line** — save / share / comment keyword.
4. **3–5 niche hashtags** — e.g. #personalfinance #moneytips #financialliteracy #sidehustle #investingforbeginners

Example (for Reel 2):
> Broke isn't a number, it's a set of habits.
> The wild part: I did all 5 of these on a good salary.
> Fixing #2 alone put $400/mo back in my pocket.
> Save this and audit yourself on the 1st of the month.
> #personalfinance #moneytips #financialliteracy
