The offer: agents send you the listing photos they already have, you turn them into
a cinematic 60-second video tour + vertical social cuts, delivered in 24 hours.
No shoot, no scheduling, no travel. You fulfill with AI animation (Higgsfield
image-to-video) + basic editing.

> **"Your listing photos → a cinematic video tour in 24 hours. No shoot needed.
> $99 launch price this week only (goes to $149)."**

**What they get:** 60s branded video tour + one 30s vertical cut for Reels/TikTok

**Why it sells:** the agent's alternative is paying $250–400 for a videographer
AND coordinating a shoot around tenants/sellers. You remove both the cost and the
scheduling. Speed is the product.

## Day-by-Day

### Day 1 (today) — Build the demo + the pipe
1. Make ONE killer demo: pick 8–10 photos you have the rights to (your own past
   shoot, a friendly agent's listing with permission, or license-free interior
   photos). Animate each into a 5s motion clip, cut to music, add titles.
   Make a 60s horizontal + 30s vertical version.
2. Set up a Stripe/PayPal payment link ($99, and a $249 three-pack).
3. Order flow = dead simple: they DM/email → you send payment link + "reply with
   10–15 photos and your logo" → deliver within 24h. No website needed this week.

### Day 2 — Outreach blitz #1 (40–50 touches)
Targets, in order of likelihood to buy **this week**:
1. **Agents with active listings that have photos but no video** — browse Zillow/
   Realtor.com for your city, filter new listings, check which have no video tour.
   These people have an immediate, findable need. Reference their exact listing.
2. **FSBO sellers** (Zillow FSBO, Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace) — they're
   marketing alone and $99 is an easy yes.
3. **Airbnb/VRBO hosts & property managers** — they already have pro photos and
   want social video. Same product, zero extra work.

### Day 3 — Follow-ups + blitz #2
- Follow up with every Day-2 non-replier (template below).

  Caption: "Made entirely from the listing photos. No shoot. 24-hour delivery.

### Day 4 — Deliver + referral loop

### Day 5 — Brokerage play

### Day 6–7 — Fulfill, follow up, convert to recurring

## Rules That Keep You Out of Trouble (read before Day 1)

1. **Only animate photos your client owns or is licensed to use.** Listing photos
   usually belong to the photographer with a marketing license to the agent —
   an agent hiring you to market their own listing is normal use, but have THEM
   send you the photos (that's their license, not scraped by you).
2. **Never scrape MLS/Zillow photos of someone else's listing** for demos or
   outreach samples. For your demo, use photos you own or get one agent's
   permission — that permission ask is itself a sales conversation.
3. **Don't add, remove, or change anything in the rooms.** Camera motion on real
   photos = video tour. Changing furniture/finishes = virtual staging, which most
   MLSs require to be disclosed. Adding a small "Created from listing photography"
   label keeps you clearly on the right side.
4. Deliver files the client can post anywhere; put your name/logo only in the
   outro unless they pay for unbranded.

## Production Pipeline (per video, ~1–2 hours total)

1. Client sends 10–15 photos → pick the best 8–10 (front exterior, kitchen,
   living, primary bed/bath, backyard, one detail shot).
2. Animate each photo into a 5s clip with slow camera motion (push-in on the
   front exterior, lateral glide through living spaces, slow rise for backyard).
3. Assemble: exterior hook first, then kitchen/living (the money rooms), bedrooms,
   outdoor space last. Music: licensed track (Artlist/Epidemic or platform-free audio).
4. Titles: address + beds/baths/sqft on the opening shot, agent branding outro.
5. Export 16:9 (60s) and 9:16 (30s, best 6 clips).

Template everything after the first one — video #2 onward should take under an hour.
