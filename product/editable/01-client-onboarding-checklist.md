# Client Onboarding Checklist

*Run this checklist every time a client says "yes." It turns a verbal agreement into a professional engagement — and sets the tone that you run a real operation.*

## Phase 1 — Lock It In (Day 0, the day they say yes)

- [ ] Send a same-day confirmation email (use Email Script #5, "Kickoff Confirmation")
- [ ] Send the contract for signature (see Contract Essentials Checklist)
- [ ] Send the deposit invoice — **do not start work before the deposit clears**
- [ ] Create the project folder (files, assets, deliverables, admin)
- [ ] Add project milestones to your calendar, including a mid-project check-in

## Phase 2 — Kickoff (within 3 business days of signed contract + deposit)

- [ ] Schedule a 30-minute kickoff call
- [ ] Send a kickoff agenda 24 hours before the call:
  - Goals recap (what success looks like, in the client's words)
  - Deliverables and dates
  - What you need from them, and by when
  - Communication plan (channel, response time, meeting cadence)
- [ ] On the call, confirm the **single point of contact** who gives final approvals
- [ ] Confirm who provides assets (logins, brand files, copy, access) and the deadline for them
- [ ] State your revision policy out loud (e.g., "Two revision rounds are included; additional rounds are billed at my hourly rate")

## Phase 3 — Access & Assets (within 5 business days)

- [ ] Collect all logins/access via a secure method (password manager share link — never plain email)
- [ ] Collect brand assets, content, and reference materials
- [ ] Log the date each item was received — **if client delays cost you time, you'll want the paper trail**
- [ ] Send a short "we're fully set up" note once everything is in

## Phase 4 — Working Rhythm (ongoing)

- [ ] Send the Weekly Status Update every Friday (Template #7)
- [ ] Log scope-change requests the moment they happen (use Email Script #6 to respond)
- [ ] Invoice on the schedule stated in the contract — never "when it feels polite"

## The 3 Rules That Make Onboarding Work

1. **No deposit, no work.** A client who resists a 25–50% deposit is showing you how they'll behave at final invoice time.
2. **Everything in writing.** Decisions made on calls get confirmed by email within 24 hours ("Recapping what we agreed today...").
3. **One approver.** Projects die when three people give conflicting feedback. Name the approver in the kickoff call and the contract.
