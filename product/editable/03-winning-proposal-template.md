# Winning Proposal Template

*Copy this structure for every quote. It sells the outcome first, presents price as a choice between options (not yes/no), and closes with a clear expiry. Replace everything in [brackets].*

---

## Proposal: [Project Name]

**Prepared for:** [Client name, Company]
**Prepared by:** [Your name]
**Date:** [Date] · **Valid until:** [Date +14 days]

### 1. What You Told Me

[2–4 sentences reflecting their situation back, using their exact words from the discovery call. This section does more selling than any other — it proves you listened.]

> Example: "You're getting consistent traffic to the site, but only 0.8% of visitors book a call. You've tried two redesigns that focused on visuals rather than conversion, and every month this continues costs you an estimated 15–20 qualified leads."

### 2. What Success Looks Like

By the end of this project, you will have:

- [Outcome 1 — in business terms, not deliverable terms]
- [Outcome 2]
- [Outcome 3]

*(Write "a checkout flow that converts above 2%", not "5 redesigned pages.")*

### 3. How We'll Get There

| Phase | What happens | You'll receive | Timing |
|-------|-------------|----------------|--------|
| 1. [Discovery/Audit] | [1 sentence] | [Deliverable] | Week 1 |
| 2. [Build/Create] | [1 sentence] | [Deliverable] | Weeks 2–3 |
| 3. [Refine/Launch] | [1 sentence] | [Deliverable] | Week 4 |

### 4. Investment

**Option A — [Core]: $[X]**
[Everything in Section 3. The complete solution to the problem discussed.]

**Option B — [Core + Growth]: $[Y, roughly 1.6–2x of A]**
Everything in Option A, plus:
- [High-value addition, e.g., 60 days of post-launch optimization]
- [Second addition]

*Most clients in your situation choose Option B, because [specific reason tied to their goal].*

**Payment terms:** [40%] to begin, [30%] at [milestone], [30%] on delivery. Payable within [7] days of invoice.

### 5. What's Included — and What's Not

**Included:** [revision rounds, e.g., "two rounds of revisions per deliverable"], project management, weekly written status updates.

**Not included (available at $[rate]/hr):** [common scope-creep items for your field — extra revision rounds, additional pages, new formats, rush turnaround].

### 6. About Me

[3–4 sentences: who you help, one relevant result with a number, one line of social proof.]

> "[Short client testimonial with a concrete result.]" — [Name, Company]

### 7. Next Step

To start, reply "Option A" or "Option B" to this email. I'll send the agreement and deposit invoice the same day, and we'll have your kickoff call within [3] business days.

This proposal is valid until **[date]**. My next available start date after that is [date +3–4 weeks].

---

## Why Each Piece Is There (delete before sending)

- **"What You Told Me" comes first** because clients skim proposals looking for evidence you understood them. Price means nothing until they trust the diagnosis.
- **Two options, not one** changes the question from "yes or no?" to "which one?" — and 30–40% of clients take the bigger option when it's framed around their goal.
- **The expiry date and next start date are real scarcity**, not fake urgency — your calendar genuinely fills. State it plainly and it motivates a decision without pressure tactics.
- **"Not included" is your anti-scope-creep insurance.** Naming the extras up front makes billing for them later a smooth conversation instead of a conflict.
